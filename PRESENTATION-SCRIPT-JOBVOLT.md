## "The Two Fastest-Growing Jobs in America Are Both in Clean Energy"
### Team: Juan, Ismael, Kevin | Saturday Presentation

### SLIDE 1 — THE HOOK (Ismael, 60 seconds)
**[Show: JobVolt hero stats bar + BLS chart]**

> "Quick question — what are the two fastest-growing jobs in America right now? Not AI. Not nursing. Not software engineering.

> According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, it's wind turbine service technician — 60% growth. And solar photovoltaic installer — 48% growth. Both in clean energy. Both pay $49K to $62K. Both require zero four-year degree.

> This isn't a niche industry. 3.5 million Americans work in clean energy today. That's more than the entire US airline industry, more than real estate, more than the movie industry. And it's growing 3x faster than the overall economy.

> $2.3 trillion was invested in clean energy globally in 2025. That money isn't theoretical — it translates directly into people getting hired to install panels, maintain turbines, wire battery systems, and upgrade the grid. The jobs are here. The question is: who's getting them?"

### SLIDE 2 — THE DATA: WHERE THE JOBS ARE (Kevin, 60 seconds)
**[Show: JobVolt "Jobs by Sector" doughnut + "State Explorer"]**

> "Let's break down what 3.5 million jobs actually looks like.

> Energy efficiency is the biggest slice — 890,000 workers. That's HVAC technicians, building retrofitters, insulation installers. Unsexy work that pays well and is in massive demand.

> Solar is next at 370,000. Electric vehicles and transport at 480,000. Grid modernization — electricians, lineworkers, substation techs — 310,000. Wind at 125,000. And the fastest-growing category: battery storage at 95,000 — a sector that barely existed five years ago.

> Now look at where they are. California leads with 500,000 clean energy jobs. Texas is second with 310,000 and growing at 18% per year — faster than California. That's not politics — it's economics. Texas has more wind than anyone and solar is exploding.

> The top 10 states hold 60% of all clean energy jobs. But the demand exists everywhere — every state needs electricians, every state has buildings to retrofit, every state has a grid to modernize."

**[Click to next slide]**

### SLIDE 3 — THE CONFLICT: THE WORKFORCE GAP (Juan, 60 seconds)
**[Show: JobVolt "What-If Simulator" + Industry Comparison Table]**

> "So here's the problem — and it's not what you'd expect.

> The #1 barrier to clean energy deployment right now is not cost. Batteries dropped 97%. Solar dropped 99%. It's not technology. It's not politics. It's people. We literally do not have enough trained workers.

> The US needs 500,000 additional clean energy workers by 2030 to hit deployment targets. Half a million people. And our training pipeline is producing maybe half of that.

> Look at this simulator. The red line is what the industry needs. The green line is what we're producing. There's a gap — and it's widening.

> Meanwhile, look at how we compare to other industries. AI pays more, but requires a CS degree and has 250,000 total jobs. Healthcare has 16 million jobs but grows at 5.8% and demands years of education. Clean energy is the only industry that combines high growth, high demand, and low entry barriers.

> 40% of current solar workers have no college degree. These are accessible, middle-class careers. And we can't fill them fast enough."

**[Click to next slide]**

### SLIDE 4 — THE RESOLUTION: THE PATHWAY IN (Ismael, 60 seconds)
**[Show: JobVolt "Career Pathways" cards + "Education Levels" chart]**

> "Here's what an actual career pathway looks like.

> Solar installer. $48,800 median. Most employers provide on-the-job training. You can start with zero experience, get a NABCEP certification in 3-6 months, and be making $50K within your first year. No college required.

> Wind turbine tech. $61,770 median. This one does benefit from a 2-year associate degree, but there are certificate programs as short as 9 months. You're climbing 300-foot towers and maintaining million-dollar machines. It's physical, technical, and it pays.

> Battery storage technician. $56,200 median. This is the newest role — it barely existed three years ago. Electrical background helps, but companies are so desperate for workers they'll hire and train.

> Energy electrician. $65,280 median. Union apprenticeships are the strongest pathway. You earn while you learn, and demand is so high that apprentices are getting placed before they graduate.

> Here's the stat that matters for everyone in this room: 40% of clean energy workers have no college degree. 22% have some college or a certificate. That's 62% of the workforce that got in without a bachelor's. This is the most accessible high-growth industry in America."

**[Click to next slide]**

### SLIDE 5 — THE CLOSE (Kevin, 60 seconds)
**[Show: JobVolt "Salary Comparison" chart + full dashboard]**

> "Let me put a fine point on this.

> A solar installer makes $48,800 — that's 45% more than a retail worker and 30% more than a warehouse worker. A wind tech at $61,770 makes more than many office jobs that required a 4-year degree.

> But it's not just about the money. It's about the trajectory. When an industry is growing at 48-60% and can't find enough workers, that means promotions. That means raises. That means job security.

> The Bureau of Labor Statistics doesn't rank solar installer #2 because it's trendy. They rank it #2 because the math says the demand is real, it's growing, and it's not going away.

> $2.3 trillion is flowing into this industry globally. Every dollar of investment creates jobs. Every solar panel needs an installer. Every battery needs a technician. Every mile of grid needs an electrician.

> The two fastest-growing jobs in America are both in clean energy. They don't require a 4-year degree. They pay middle-class wages. And there are half a million positions we can't fill.

> The workforce gap is the opportunity. Our live dashboard is at jobvolt on GitHub Pages.

> Thank you."

## IF THEY ASK QUESTIONS
- **"Are these jobs going to last or is it a bubble?"** — $2.3T in global investment, BLS projects growth through 2033, and the physics don't change — every panel needs installation, every turbine needs maintenance. This is infrastructure, not a fad.
- **"What about AI replacing these jobs?"** — You can't have an AI climb a wind turbine or install a solar panel on a roof. These are physical, skilled trades. AI might help with design and monitoring, but the hands-on work is human.
- **"Can you really support a family on $49K?"** — $49K is the starting point. Median is $49-62K. Senior techs and crew leads make $70-85K. Electricians with experience clear $80K+. And these jobs come with benefits — the majority are full-time with health insurance.
- **"Why not just go into tech?"** — Tech is great if you have a CS degree. But 62% of Americans don't have a bachelor's. Clean energy is the highest-growth industry accessible to that 62%. It's not either/or — it's about meeting people where they are.
- **"What's the Pursuit connection?"** — Pursuit exists to create pathways to upward mobility for underrepresented communities. Clean energy is the largest new source of accessible, middle-class jobs in America. The overlap is direct.
