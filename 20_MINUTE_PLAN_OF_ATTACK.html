⚡ 20-Minute Plan of Attack

Data Analysis Sprint → Pitch Preparation

⏱️ 20 MINUTES UNTIL PRESSURE TEST

📖 Quick Reference: What This Plan Does

This 20-minute sprint takes you from "we have data" to "we found something that matters and here's what we'd build to fix it."

The Goal:

  • Find the strongest pattern or problem in your data
  • Articulate WHY it matters (impact in dollars, efficiency, time, people affected)
  • Sketch what SOLUTION could address it
  • Get ready to defend it when another group pressure-tests you at 9:10

Why This Matters:

Companies don't buy reports. They buy SOLUTIONS to problems. By having a clear problem + insight + solution concept, you're already thinking like an entrepreneur, not just a data analyst.

🎯 The Real Goal:
You're not trying to be perfect. You're trying to show: "Here's what we found, here's why it matters, and yes, we've thought about what could fix it."
1

Rapid Data Analysis (0-5 minutes)

5 MIN

What You Need Right Now

Translation: You have 5 minutes to quickly scan ALL your visualizations and spot what stands out. Don't analyze everything—find the MOST interesting thing first.

  • Scan all your visualizations - Line charts, bar charts, distributions
  • Look for anomalies - Weird things that don't fit the pattern (e.g., one huge spike while everything else is stable)
  • Find the biggest change - Most dramatic shift: biggest spike up/down, fastest decline
  • Identify correlations - When X increases, Y also increases (they move together)
  • Note outliers - Data points that are WAY different from everything else
💡 Key Questions to Ask Your Data:
"What changed most dramatically? What pattern repeats? What's broken? What's working?"

Quick Metrics Scan

  • What's the highest value? When did it occur?
  • What's the lowest value? When did it occur?
  • What's the trend direction? Up, down, or flat?
  • What's the volatility? Stable or chaotic?
  • Are there seasonality patterns? Cyclical behaviors?
2

Identify Your #1 Insight (5-10 minutes)

5 MIN

The ONE Thing

You need a single, powerful insight. Not five. Not three. ONE that makes people say "Oh damn, that's important."

Why this matters: When you pitch on Saturday, you need ONE thing people remember. Not 5 things. ONE clear statement.

The difference:

  • ❌ Bad: "We looked at solar panel data and found lots of patterns"
  • ✅ Good: "Solar panels lose 15% efficiency in summer heat peaks, costing $2M annually"

❌ GAPS / PROBLEMS

  • Inefficiencies
  • Wasted resources
  • Missed opportunity
  • System breaking
  • Revenue leaking

✅ OPPORTUNITIES

  • Untapped potential
  • Quick win
  • Efficiency gain
  • Growth area
  • Cost savings

How to Frame Your Insight

  • "We discovered..." [Specific finding from your data]
  • "This means..." [Why it matters for renewable energy]
  • "The impact is..." [Money? Efficiency? Safety? Speed?]
  • "We could solve this by..." [High-level solution direction]
🎯 Example Pattern:
"We discovered that solar panel efficiency drops 15% during peak heat hours in summer. This represents ~$2M in lost revenue annually. We could solve this by building a predictive cooling system that identifies optimal times for maintenance."
3

Deep Dive: Gaps & Inefficiencies (10-15 minutes)

5 MIN

Ask These Questions About Your Data

1. WHERE ARE THE INEFFICIENCIES?

Translation: What processes are WASTING resources right now?

  • What processes are losing energy/money/time?
  • Where's the biggest bottleneck (what's slowing things down)?
  • What's being wasted or underutilized?
  • What takes too long?

Real example: "Wind turbines are only operating 60% of the time due to poorly scheduled maintenance"

2. WHAT PROBLEMS EXIST?

Translation: Where is your data showing failure or decline? What's costing the company money?

  • Where is the data showing failure or decline?
  • What makes the company lose money?
  • What customer/operational pain point does this reveal?
  • What's getting worse (negative trends)?
3. WHERE'S THE OPPORTUNITY?

Translation: Where could a 20% improvement = huge impact? Which groups are doing well that we could copy?

  • Which segment/region is underperforming? (Could improve)
  • Which segment is overperforming? (Can we replicate?)
  • What's NOT being measured that should be?
  • Where could a 20% improvement = huge impact?

Real example: "That 40% downtime could generate $5M if we improve scheduling"

The Problem Statement

Write it in 1 sentence:

"[Company name] is losing $X annually because [specific problem revealed by data]. This affects [who] and impacts [what metric]."

Your data should support every claim here.

4

Build Concept Sketch (15-20 minutes)

5 MIN

What Could You Build?

Don't overthink this. You're not building the whole thing in 5 minutes. You're answering: "What's the simplest tool that would fix this problem?"

Key insight: You're not just identifying problems. You're showing you could actually SOLVE them. That's what makes you stand out.

Don't jump to complexity. Could a better dashboard solve it? A phone alert? Smarter scheduling? Start simple, then layer complexity.

Solution Format

  • Product Name: What would you call it?
  • What it does: 1-2 sentence description
  • The problem it solves: Link back to your insight
  • How it works: Simple explanation
  • Impact: How much better does this make things?

Types of Solutions for Renewable Energy

Pick one of these categories and sketch your concept:

  • Dashboard/Monitoring Tool - Real-time visibility into problem areas (see issues as they happen)
  • Prediction Algorithm - Forecast issues BEFORE they happen (prevent problems)
  • Optimization System - Automatically improve efficiency (work smarter)
  • Segmentation Strategy - Custom solutions for different regions/customers (one-size doesn't fit all)
  • Automation - Reduce manual processes that waste time (save human effort)
  • Mobile App - Field teams get instant insights instead of waiting for reports (faster decisions)
💡 Think Simple First:
Don't jump to AI/ML. Could a better dashboard solve it? A phone alert? Smarter scheduling? Start simple, then layer complexity.

The One-Liner Pitch

Fill this in:

"We're building a [tool/system/app] that [solves problem] for [renewable energy company], which could reduce [cost/time/waste] by [X%] and save [money/time/carbon]."

🔥 At 9:10: Pressure Test Incoming

Another group will challenge your analysis. This is NOT personal—they're testing your thinking. How well you defend = how credible your pitch will be on Saturday.

They WILL ask:
  • "How confident are you in this data?" (Translation: Is your data reliable?)
  • "Did you consider [other factor]?" (Translation: Did you think about competing explanations?)
  • "What if we're wrong about [assumption]?" (Translation: What could break your theory?)
  • "Why is THIS the top priority over [other problem]?" (Translation: Why should we care about THIS and not something else?)
  • "How would you actually build this?" (Translation: Show you've thought it through)
  • "What's the ROI?" (Translation: How much money/value are we talking about?)
Your prepared answers:
  • Be ready to defend your #1 insight (know your numbers cold: "15%" not "a lot")
  • Have counter-questions ready ("What would make this MORE important?")
  • Know what you DON'T know and admit it ("This assumes X, which we couldn't test")
  • Have 2-3 backup insights ready if they poke holes in your main one
  • Be specific with numbers—they matter (not "a lot" but "15%" or "$2M")
5

Saturday Pitch: The Thread You're Pulling

PREP NOW

Build Your Narrative Arc

Your Saturday pitch is a STORY, not a data dump. Take people on a journey:

  • Act 1: "Here's the renewable energy problem we looked at" (Why should they care?)
  • Act 2: "Our data revealed this critical insight" (Show visualizations, tell the story)
  • Act 3: "We could solve it with this build" (Solution concept)
  • Act 4: "Here's the impact" (Numbers matter: money, time, efficiency gains)
🎬 The Hook:
Start with the INSIGHT, not the data. "We discovered that X" gets attention. "Here's a line chart" does not.

What to Have Ready by Saturday

  • 3-4 key visualizations that tell your story
  • 1 clear problem statement with numbers
  • 1 product/solution concept
  • Impact estimate (how much better/cheaper/faster)
  • Answer to: "Why should they care?"

⭐ NEXT 20 MINUTES: FOCUS SEQUENCE

Min 0-5: Scan data for anomalies & patterns — Find the strongest signal

Min 5-10: Identify your #1 insight (the ONE thing) — Not 5 things, just 1

Min 10-15: Articulate the problem it reveals — Why should they care?

Min 15-20: Sketch a simple solution concept — What could fix this?

Then: Show up confident to pressure test. Be ready to defend, learn, and iterate.

🎯 The Complete Flow:

Data → Anomalies → Top Insight → Problem Statement → Solution Concept → Pressure Test → Saturday Pitch

The thread you pull is: Problem → Insight → Solution. Make it tight and compelling.

Remember: The best insights are SIMPLE. If you can't explain it in one sentence, you don't understand it yet.
