CORE PROBLEMS

1. FINANCIAL BARRIERS
   - Annual funding gap: $190 billion
   - Required investment by 2030: $600 billion
   - Current available: $410 billion
   - Battery costs remain high for widespread deployment
   - Tax incentives are changing, creating uncertainty
   - Investment activity declining (deal volume down 89% in early 2025)

2. REGULATORY & POLICY CHALLENGES
   - Interconnection backlog: 377 GW of approved projects waiting
   - Credit risk: 83% of planned storage may lose tax incentives under new rules
   - Policy instability: Rules changing, reducing investor confidence
   - Regional inconsistency: 10+ different regulatory frameworks across the US

3. TECHNICAL GAPS
   - Current technology: 2-4 hour battery storage
   - Required capability: 8-24+ hour storage systems
   - Efficiency loss: 5-15% per charge/discharge cycle
   - Deployment gap: Have 37.4 GW installed, need 187 GW by 2030
   - Emerging technology still developing (not yet production-ready)

HOW WE KNOW THIS IS HAPPENING NOW

These aren't historical problems—they're measurable right now.

StorageScout (real-time monitoring platform) tracks solar generation across 10 major US battery storage states every 60 seconds. It quantifies:

1. Live Solar Generation (W/m²)
   - Direct + diffuse radiation measured in real-time
   - Updates hourly across CA, TX, AZ, NV, FL, NY, IL, GA, VA, CO
   - Shows exactly when solar peaks and when batteries are needed

2. Real-Time Curtailment Calculation
   - Formula: (Current Solar Radiation / 800) × (Historical Curtailment / 2000) × (3000 / Existing Storage MW)
   - States with high solar + low storage relative to history = wasting energy RIGHT NOW
   - Updated every 60 seconds; visible opportunity windows throughout the day

3. Opportunity Scoring (0-100)
   - Red (60-100): Storage urgently needed at this moment
   - Yellow (30-60): Moderate storage opportunity
   - Green (0-30): Adequate storage for current conditions
   - Score changes hourly based on actual generation vs. storage capacity

What this means for customer conversations:

- Instead of saying "Storage is growing exponentially":
  "Arizona had 614 W/m² solar at 2 PM yesterday and only 2,100 MW storage. Right now you're wasting peak solar every clear day."

- Instead of saying "83% of projects are at credit risk":
  "I can show you the exact hours each week when high solar + low storage = missing revenue. This is how I know where to deploy storage."

- Instead of saying "Interconnection backlog is 377 GW":
  "That 377 GW backlog means approved projects can't connect. But the existing 37.4 GW is underutilized. I'm solving the optimization problem first."

This real-time data turns abstract market statistics into visible, measurable, hourly business opportunities.
